The Training Program

Six days that can change the direction of your career

Beyond & Above has trained corporate flight attendants and pilots for over 24 years at Fort Lauderdale Executive Airport — one of the most active private aviation hubs in the United States. Every course includes hands-on time aboard real luxury jets at Banyan Air Service's FBO.

The initial 6-day program covers emergency safety procedures, white glove service delivery, culinary arts and galley management, cabin etiquette, passenger relations, and resume preparation. No prior experience is required. Students leave with the skills, certifications, and confidence to begin working immediately.

Graduates routinely receive job offers within weeks. Corporate flight attendants on FAA Part 135 operators typically earn $600 to $900 per day. The investment in training pays for itself quickly — and the lifestyle it opens is unlike anything else in aviation.

What you will learn

The curriculum

01 — Emergency Safety

Emergency procedures training aligned with FAA Parts 91, 125, and 135. Students train on fire suppression, emergency evacuations, ditching procedures, first aid, and defibrillator use — all aboard actual aircraft at Banyan Air Service's FBO at Fort Lauderdale Executive Airport.

02 — White Glove Service

The service standards expected aboard private jets are unlike anything in commercial aviation. Students master high-end cabin protocols, anticipatory service, guest preferences, in-flight etiquette, and the nuanced demands of ultra-high-net-worth principals and their families.

03 — Culinary & Galley

Students learn aircraft galley management, catering logistics, special dietary needs, table setting, food safety at altitude, and the art of delivering a restaurant-quality experience in a private aircraft cabin.

04 — Career Placement

The program does not end at graduation. Students receive professional resume writing tailored to the corporate aviation market, interview coaching, and ongoing support from the Beyond & Above team. Instructors remain a resource long after class ends.
